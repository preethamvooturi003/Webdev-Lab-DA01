1931: Born

Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam was born on October 15, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu.

1954: Graduation

He graduated in Physics from Saint Joseph's College, Tiruchirappalli, in 1954.

1960: Aeronautical Engineering

Dr. Kalam gained a degree in Aeronautical Engineering from Madras Institute of Technology.

1969: ISRO

He transferred to ISRO from the DRDO.

1980: SLV-III

India entered the space club by putting the Rohini satellite in near-earth orbit with the first indigenous Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III), developed under the stewardship of Dr. Kalam.

1980s-1990s: Missile Development

As the chief of the Integrated Guided Missile Development Programme, he was responsible for the development and operationalization of AGNI and PRITHVI Missiles.

1992-1999: Chief Scientific Advisor

Dr. Kalam worked as Chief Scientific Advisor to the PM and Secretary of the DRDO.

1998: Pokhran II

India conducted the Pokhran II nuclear tests in May with Dr. Kalam as the chief project coordinator.

1999-2001: Principal Scientific Advisor

He served as the Principal Scientific Advisor to the Government of India.

2002-2007: President of India

Dr. Kalam served as the President of India during this period.

2015: Passing Away

He passed away on July 27, 2015, after collapsing during a lecture at the Indian Institute of Management in Shillong.
